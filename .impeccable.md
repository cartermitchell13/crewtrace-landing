# Crewtrace Design System

## Design Context

### Users
Construction company owners, operations managers, and field supervisors who need to track crew time, location, and compliance. They are practical, time-pressed, and skeptical of software — they want immediate clarity on what the product does and proof that it works. The interface should evoke **confidence** and **trust** without feeling corporate or cold.

### Brand Personality
**Authoritative, Precise, Clean.** Crewtrace speaks with quiet confidence — no hype, no fluff. Every pixel should communicate reliability and professionalism. The brand respects its users' intelligence and time.

### Aesthetic Direction

#### Primary References (from Refero analysis)
- **Cohere** (`cohere.com`): Clean white backgrounds, large serif headings, sans-serif body text, centered hero with clear CTAs, logo walls, tabbed feature sections, two-column content blocks, gradient accent blobs (purple/orange), dark footer, generous 40-60px vertical spacing between sections
- **Missive** (`missiveapp.com`): Black-and-white palette with bright accent CTAs, serif headings (Tiempos Headline) + sans-serif body (Inter), single-column vertical scroll, large product screenshots in device frames, logo wall in monochrome, testimonial carousel with star ratings, bento grid layouts, dark footer

#### Key Design Patterns to Adopt
1. **Typographic contrast**: Serif headings + sans-serif body (like both references)
2. **Predominantly white** background with high-contrast black text
3. **Minimal, professional aesthetic** — generous whitespace, no clutter
4. **Large product screenshots/mockups** as hero and feature visuals
5. **Logo walls** for social proof (monochrome logos)
6. **Segmented sections** with ample vertical rhythm (80-120px between major sections)
7. **Simple navigation** — logo left, links center/right, clear primary CTA
8. **Dark footer** with multi-column links
9. **Rounded corners** on cards (12-16px, not the current 2rem-2.5rem)
10. **Subtle shadows** for depth, not hard offset neobrutalist shadows

#### Anti-References (what Crewtrace should NOT be)
- Overly playful or colorful startup aesthetics
- Boring enterprise software (gray, dense, lifeless)
- Dark/techy developer tools aesthetic
- Generic template / stock-photo heavy

#### Theme
Light mode only.

### Button Styles

**Primary CTA**: `bg-primary`, white text, `rounded-xl`, no offset shadow, subtle `translateY(-1px)` on hover with slight shadow lift.

**Secondary CTA**: Transparent/white background, dark border, dark text, same radius. On hover, light background fill.

**Ghost/Link CTA**: Text-only with arrow icon, underline on hover (like Cohere's "Try the playground").

### Component Patterns

**Navigation**: Minimal horizontal bar. Logo left. Links center-right. Primary CTA button right. Sticky on scroll with subtle backdrop blur.

**Hero Section**: Large centered serif heading. Short supporting paragraph in body font. Two CTAs side by side (primary + secondary/ghost). Below: large product screenshot in a subtle device frame or rounded container.

**Logo Wall**: Horizontal row of grayscale/monochrome client logos. Evenly spaced. Centered. Label above: "Trusted by" or similar.

**Feature Sections**: Two-column layout — text on one side, screenshot/image on other. Alternating sides. Or: three-column icon+heading+description grid.

**Testimonials**: Quote text, attribution with photo, company logo. Star rating if available. Carousel or single featured.

**CTA Banner**: Full-width section. Can use primary color background with white text, or dark background with accent text. Clear heading + CTA button.

**Footer**: Dark background. Multi-column link groups. Logo. Copyright. Social icons.

### Design Principles

1. **Clarity over cleverness** — Every element should immediately communicate its purpose. No decorative noise.
2. **Let the product speak** — Large, clear screenshots and mockups are more persuasive than illustrations or abstract graphics.
3. **Typographic hierarchy drives the eye** — Serif headings create gravitas; sans-serif body ensures readability. The contrast between the two creates rhythm.
4. **Whitespace is a feature** — Generous spacing between sections signals quality and confidence. Never crowd the page.
5. **Restrained color** — The palette is almost monochromatic (black/white/gray) with purple as the sole accent. This restraint makes the CTAs and highlights pop harder.
