## The Growing Problem with Manual Tracking

As project volume increased, each Chen Construction site manager handled attendance a little differently. That made weekly labor reporting inconsistent and created friction during subcontractor reconciliation.

The office team had no reliable way to answer basic questions quickly: who was on Site A at 7:10 AM, who moved to Site C after lunch, and which subcontractor hours were confirmed versus estimated.

## Implementing the Crewtrace Framework

Chen Construction launched geofenced attendance rules per active job and made the central dashboard the source of truth for both office and field leadership.

They paired that rollout with a weekly variance review. Instead of catching issues at payroll close, managers reviewed exceptions throughout the week and resolved them while details were still fresh.

## Tangible Business Impact

The team removed over six hours of manual payroll processing every week, but the operational gain was larger than time savings. Project managers could now see labor movement in near real time and manage staffing with less guesswork.

Disputes with subcontractors were resolved faster because conversations started from verified records, not reconstructed notes.
