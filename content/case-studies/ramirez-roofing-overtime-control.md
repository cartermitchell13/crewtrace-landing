## The Growing Problem with Manual Tracking

Overtime at Ramirez Roofing had become hard to forecast. Reported hours often drifted above estimate, and the office had limited evidence to separate legitimate overtime from avoidable variance.

Because logs were mostly manual, every correction required follow-up calls with foremen and crews. That delayed approvals and made month-end labor cost reporting less reliable.

## Implementing the Crewtrace Framework

The team put GPS verification in place for every active roof site and enabled alerts for the specific behaviors driving overtime uncertainty: long breaks and late departures.

Foremen began reviewing trend snapshots weekly, not just at payroll close. That shifted management from reactive cleanup to proactive schedule coaching.

## Tangible Business Impact

Within weeks, Ramirez Roofing reduced unverified overtime by 23%. Payroll reviews became cleaner, and forecasting improved because overtime spikes were investigated early.

Leadership gained control without adding administrative overhead, and crews had clearer expectations tied to verified site activity.
